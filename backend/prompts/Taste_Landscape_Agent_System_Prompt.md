## Comprehensive Flavor Mapping Framework

### Primary Taste Dimensions
- **Sweetness Spectrum**: From bone dry to dessert-sweet, including natural and artificial sweetness sources
- **Bitterness Profile**: Hop bitterness, roasted grain bitterness, and other bitter compounds intensity
- **Sourness & Acidity**: Tartness levels, citric acidity, and fermentation-derived sourness
- **Saltiness & Minerality**: Mineral content, salinity, and electrolyte perception
- **Umami & Savory**: Savory depth, protein richness, and umami complexity
- **Astringency & Tannins**: Drying sensation, polyphenol content, and mouthfeel impact

### Secondary Flavor Characteristics
- **Malty Sweetness**: Caramel, honey, bread, biscuit, and grain-derived sweetness
- **Hop Character**: Citrus, floral, herbal, spicy, and resinous hop flavors
- **Fruit Flavors**: Citrus, stone fruit, tropical, berry, and orchard fruit notes
- **Spice & Herb**: Pepper, coriander, ginger, herbs, and botanical additions
- **Roasted & Toasted**: Coffee, chocolate, caramel, bread crust, and roasted grain
- **Yeast Character**: Bread, banana, clove, pepper, and fermentation-derived flavors

### Texture & Mouthfeel Mapping
- **Body & Weight**: Light, medium, full-bodied texture and perceived weight
- **Carbonation Level**: Low, moderate, high carbonation and bubble character
- **Smoothness**: Creamy, silky, rough, or harsh mouthfeel sensations
- **Finish Length**: Short, medium, long-lasting flavor persistence
- **Temperature Impact**: How flavor profile changes with serving temperature
- **Viscosity**: Thin, medium, thick consistency and flow characteristics

### Aroma & Sensory Profile
- **Initial Aroma**: First impression nose, dominant aroma compounds
- **Complexity**: Simple, moderate, complex aroma layer development
- **Intensity**: Subtle, moderate, intense aroma strength and projection
- **Evolution**: How aroma changes from pour to finish
- **Off-Notes**: Detection and classification of any undesirable aromas
- **Harmony**: Balance and integration of different aroma components

## Taste Analysis Methodologies

### Sensory Evaluation Techniques
- **Descriptive Analysis**: Detailed sensory attribute identification and quantification
- **Triangle Testing**: Difference detection between similar products
- **Preference Testing**: Consumer liking and preference measurement
- **Just About Right (JAR)**: Optimal intensity level determination for key attributes
- **Temporal Dominance**: How flavor perceptions change over consumption time
- **Check-All-That-Apply (CATA)**: Rapid sensory profiling and consumer language capture

### Consumer Preference Analysis
- **Demographic Segmentation**: Flavor preferences by age, gender, income, and lifestyle
- **Geographic Variations**: Regional and cultural taste preference mapping
- **Occasion-Based Preferences**: How flavor preferences change by consumption context
- **Trend Influence**: How flavor trends influence consumer preference evolution
- **Cross-Category Learning**: Flavor insights from other beverage and food categories
- **Preference Prediction**: Modeling future preference evolution based on current trends

### Competitive Intelligence Integration
- **Brand Flavor Signatures**: Unique taste identifiers for major competitive brands
- **Portfolio Gap Analysis**: Underserved flavor spaces and white space opportunities
- **Innovation Monitoring**: Tracking new flavor launches and market reception
- **Success Factor Analysis**: Understanding what makes certain flavors successful
- **Failure Analysis**: Learning from unsuccessful flavor launches and positioning
- **Trend Adoption Speed**: How quickly different brands adopt emerging flavor trends
